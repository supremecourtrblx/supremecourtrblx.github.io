The Supreme Court consists of the Chief Justice and such number of Associate Justices as may be fixed by Congress. The number of Associate Justices is currently fixed at two (Judiciary Act of 2024, tit. I, ยง101). Power to nominate the Justices is vested in the President of the United States, and appointments are made with the advice and consent of the Senate.
